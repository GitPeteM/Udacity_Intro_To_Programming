Cactus Wren

Cactus Wren rarely drink water as they get majority of their liquids from insects and fruit. A true scavenger.

  • Scientific Name: Campylorhynchus brunneicapillus
  • Length/Weight: 8 in & 1.7 oz
  • State Bird: Arizona
  • Nesting: Cacti & Shrubs

Cactus Wrens thrive in desert habitats. They are fearless birds of the desert that announce their presence atop cacti and forage in the open. A Cactus Wren will build it's nest ontop of a cactus and typical eat insects.
